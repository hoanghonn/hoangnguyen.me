I came to trail running late — I was 26 when I ran my first 5km on dirt, and I didn't register for my first trail race until this year. The 50KM distance felt both audacious and achievable, which is probably the right level of ambition for a first attempt.

## Training

Four months of base-building. Two long runs per week, one of which was always on trails. I used power hiking as a genuine training tool, not a failure mode — uphills at sustainable effort, run everything flat and downhill.

## Race Day

The start was cold. By km 15, the fog had burned off and the ridgeline views opened up. I ran with a small group for the middle 20km, which kept the pace honest without pushing too hard.

## Lessons

- Poles would have saved my quads on the descent. Getting them before the 70km.
- Aid station food is real food. Eat it.
- The last 10km of a 50km is a different sport than the first 40km.

Finish time: 8:17. Happy with it. Ready for more.
